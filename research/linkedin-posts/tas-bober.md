## Main Idea
This post argues that strong LinkedIn writing starts with a clear, valuable, and distinctive point of view. Tas Bober emphasizes that posting consistently is not enough if the underlying perspective is generic, weak, or easily replaceable. To stand out, creators need a point of view they can be known for.

## Why It Matters for This Project
This post is relevant to LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy for B2B SaaS because effective organic content depends not only on consistency, but also on distinctiveness. In competitive B2B environments, especially where AI can generate generic content quickly, having a clear and recognizable point of view helps experts and brands stand out, build trust, and create memorable content.

## Key Takeaways
1. A clear point of view is the foundation of strong LinkedIn content.
2. The point of view must be both valuable and distinct to make someone memorable.
3. Generic content is easier to ignore, especially in an environment shaped by AI-generated repetition.
4. A strong point of view can be developed, tested, and refined rather than guessed.
5. Distinctive positioning helps creators become known for something specific on LinkedIn.
