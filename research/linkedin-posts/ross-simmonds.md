## Main Idea
This post argues that content creation alone is not enough. Ross Simmonds emphasizes that content marketing requires an intentional distribution system in which strong content is repurposed, reposted, reformatted, and shared across multiple channels so it can keep creating value over time.

## Why It Matters for This Project
This post is highly relevant to LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy for B2B SaaS because it shows that LinkedIn success does not come only from publishing new posts. Organic growth also depends on how content is redistributed, adapted for different formats, and repeatedly surfaced to the right audience. It also supports the idea that LinkedIn can be the starting point for content-market fit before expansion into other channels.

## Key Takeaways
1. Content marketing does not end at publication; distribution is a core part of the work.
2. Repurposing and reposting are necessary because most of the audience will not see a post the first time.
3. Distribution should extend beyond one platform and follow where the audience actually spends time.
4. A repeatable distribution system can turn one strong asset into a long-term campaign.
5. Teams should master one channel first, especially LinkedIn, before expanding into broader multi-channel distribution.
